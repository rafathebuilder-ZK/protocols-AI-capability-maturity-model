## Argument

The same as the blog post, but fully developed: AI adoption failure is a protocol failure, and the unit of maturity is an organization's ability to govern uncertainty productively. This document gives deployment managers and executives a specific, usable model — not a framework they need to interpret, but a diagnostic tool they can apply to their own organization this week.

---

## Proposed structure

### 1. The problem with existing AI maturity models (~1 page)
What they measure (adoption rate, tool coverage, security controls) and what they miss (governance of uncertainty, protocol coherence between humans and AI systems). Why this matters: organizations can graduate to "Level 4" on a conventional CMM while still experiencing the failure modes that make AI adoption painful.

### 2. A protocol-framed model (~1 page)
What a capability maturity model looks like when the unit is governance of uncertainty rather than process compliance. The five-level structure, explained as a progression of protocol sophistication.

### 3. The five levels (~5–6 pages, ~1 page per level)
For each level:
- **Name and one-line description**
- **What it looks like:** Observable signs — what an outside observer would see
- **The characteristic tension:** What's pulling the organization in two directions at this level
- **The failure modes:** What happens if the organization stays stuck here
- **What progression requires:** The specific change that unlocks the next level

### 4. How to use this model (~1 page)
How to place your organization on the model. What the placement is useful for (planning, resourcing, organizational conversations). What it doesn't tell you.

### 5. Where organizations are now (~0.5 pages)
Current empirical evidence on the distribution — where most organizations actually sit. What the evidence suggests about the most common stuck points.

## Level name candidates (working, not final)

| Level | Working name | Characteristic state |
|-------|-------------|---------------------|
| 1 | Shadow | Unplanned adoption; AI in use, governance absent |
| 2 | Sanctioned | Approved tools, no consistent practice |
| 3 | Governed | Policies exist; enforcement is inconsistent |
| 4 | Integrated | AI workflows are designed, not improvised |
| 5 | Compounding | Organizational learning from AI use feeds back into capability |

*These names are working hypotheses. Research phase should test whether they map to observable reality.*
