Big "wall of text" energy. Desperately needs anchor graphics. Make the pyramid diagram, in 5 different versions with different levels highlighted in each. Make a master diagram with hover text boxes explaining levels.

Separate the levels pages into individual pages.

Really shorten the response options to short phrases and move details into smaller text. People shouldn't have to read entire paragraphs for 3 options. And needs at least 10 questions that drill into finer-grained behaviors and symptoms, not subjective vibes.

A big strength in how you guys are thinking about this is creative and original mental models and terminology. Use it. Archival vs carnival for eg. Teach the language and cmm literacy

People will be starting with tools. Put in an "evaluate your tooling" assessment that slots things by maturity level. Eg Claude Code i think is Designed while Copilot and Cursor feel more like Sanctioned and retail ChatGPT is Shadow.

It's not obvious why the website is called protocolized.dev. Add a tagline: Protocols for AI adoption

Put an about page on top menu where you introduce the SIG and name the members/contrubutors. with photos. Explain the link to protocolized mag and sop (soon to be: protocol institute)

ah this is a good one. Also feel like we have to add something about kitification. Earlier you build sophisticated internal software, now you develop software kits (combination of design choices, harnesses) that can be used to build bespoke tools. Kits as the intermediate state between AI tooling and bespokeness

Case study page as top level menu item even if only 3 paras

Stretch Idea: Lexicon page with language for mental models: Bespokification, orientation loss, execution paralysis, vibe coding… make each a linkable anchor tag. Make definitions slightly funny

Along with tooling assessment, in main assessment, repeat questions in the context of major functions: engineering, marketing sales. OR put in a note suggesting orgs should have all major functional leads take the test and share and that you guys are happy to hop on a call to discuss.

CTA after taking the test should be to send it to an org peer and/or schedule a call to discuss results with y'all. Put in calendly link or email perhaps.

The SIG brand is high-value neutrality. You're not vendors shilling particular products or big 3 consulting selling reports or expensive engagements. You're like a W3C working group or something. Protocol pros contributing to an open knowledge commons. Lean into that positioning. Avoid looking like Gartner.

Embed an html slide deck that is primarily visual and short phrases. No text longer than a fortune cookie.

Not a designer but the color palette and fonts seem lacking in freshness/popping. LinkedIn boilerplate vibes. Get a designer or a design skill in Claude to refresh the design to a better on which direction corporate aesthetics are going. I feel we're overdue for an exit out of whatever design era we're in rn. It's some unholy mix of Notion notebook site and Big Essay. Like that AI 2026 site, try to go beyond whatever the hell that design style is. Maybe add a bit of sprezzatura/flair. Things have seemed too austerly platonic lately.
