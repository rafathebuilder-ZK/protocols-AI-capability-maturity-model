# Blog Post — Brief and Plan

**Publication:** Protocolized (Substack)
**Target length:** ~1,500 words
**Status:** Not started — awaiting model development

---

## Argument

Most organizations frame AI adoption as a tooling problem. It isn't. It's a protocol problem: the rules governing how humans and AI systems interact are undefined, inconsistent, or wrong — and that's what's producing shadow adoption, quality failures, and trust collapse. A capability maturity model that names this correctly generates different and more actionable guidance than one built around security checklists or adoption rates.

---

## Proposed structure

1. **Hook (~200 words):** The shadow adoption paradox. Every organization is already at Level 1 whether they planned to be or not. The question is whether the chaos is governed or not.

2. **The diagnosis (~300 words):** Why existing AI CMMs miss the point. They measure adoption, not governance of uncertainty. The failure mode they don't see: organizations graduating from shadow use to sanctioned use without ever solving the underlying coordination problem.

3. **The model, briefly (~400 words):** Introduce the 5 levels — just enough to give the reader a map. Names, one-sentence descriptions, the characteristic tension at each level. No deep dive — that's the litepaper's job.

4. **What this changes (~400 words):** What a protocol-framed maturity model tells you that a tooling-framed one doesn't. One concrete example per level of what "protocol failure" looks like in practice.

5. **Close + pointer (~200 words):** Where most organizations actually are right now (Level 1–2 per the evidence). What it takes to move. Pointer to litepaper for the full model.
