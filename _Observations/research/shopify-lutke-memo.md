# Shopify — Lütke "non-optional" AI memo (April 2025)

## Summary
Shopify CEO Tobias Lütke issued an internal memo in April 2025 establishing AI use as a baseline expectation of employment at Shopify. The memo was widely reported and is remembered chiefly for Lütke's phrasing that opting out of learning to apply AI was "not feasible." Shopify reported 30% YoY revenue growth for full-year 2025 and a drop in operating expenses from 45% to 35% of revenue over the same period.

## Why it matters for the CMM
Canonical Level 2 case: mandate without workflow design. Lütke's memo signals strategic intent and creates organization-wide sanction to use AI, but the policy move is at the level of expectation, not at the level of designed workflow. It exemplifies the Level-2 pattern where access and sanction precede the governing protocols that would make broad adoption reliably valuable. The revenue figures are a counterweight — they do not attribute growth to the mandate, but they prevent the case from being read as a simple failure arc.
