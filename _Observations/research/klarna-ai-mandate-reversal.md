# Klarna AI mandate and reversal (2024–2025)

## Summary
In February 2024, Klarna announced an AI customer-service assistant handling the work of 700 agents. Sebastian Siemiatkowski reported 90% daily employee adoption of generative AI tools internally. By mid-2025 Klarna publicly reversed course, began rehiring human agents, and Siemiatkowski described AI-produced customer service as "generic, repetitive, and insufficiently nuanced." The company framed the reversal as a quality problem, not a cost problem.

## Why it matters for the CMM
Canonical Level-2 (Sanctioned) failure arc. The organization granted broad AI access and signaled strategic intent (the headline, the 700-agent figure, the CEO framing) before designing the governing protocol for customer interaction quality. High adoption rates and early productivity gains masked a governance gap: there was no defined review process for AI-generated customer context, no quality standard for nuance, no escalation path when the output was subtly wrong. Level-2 failure surfaced only when customers felt the degradation.

## Claims citable from this observation

- Siemiatkowski reported 90% daily adoption of generative AI among Klarna employees

- The public framing of the reversal was quality, not cost
