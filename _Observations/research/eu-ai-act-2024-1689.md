# EU AI Act — Regulation (EU) 2024/1689

## Summary
The European Union's AI Act, adopted by the European Parliament and Council on 13 June 2024 and published in the Official Journal on 12 July 2024. Entered into force 1 August 2024. Prohibitions became enforceable on 2 February 2025; most obligations (including those on high-risk AI systems) apply from 2 August 2026. High-risk categories include hiring/HR management systems, credit scoring, critical infrastructure, and medical devices. Penalties reach up to €35M or 7% of global turnover for prohibited practices, and €15M or 3% for other violations. General-purpose AI models, including LLMs, have been subject to transparency and documentation requirements since August 2025.

## Why it matters for the CMM
Global compliance forcing function for Levels 2 and above. The August 2026 effective date for high-risk AI obligations — particularly in hiring and HR — turns the documented-human-oversight requirement into a concrete deadline. At Level 2, the Act creates a regulatory failure mode: unsanctioned or weakly governed deployment of a high-risk AI system becomes a fineable event. At Levels 3 and 4, it shapes which protocols are required (documented oversight, logging, risk management). At Level 5, the Act provides the statutory lattice through which planet-scale AI governance is likely to develop in the EU's jurisdictional reach.
