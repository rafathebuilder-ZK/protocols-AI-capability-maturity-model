# Duolingo — contractor replacement and AI-first backlash (2024–2025)

## Summary
Duolingo publicly moved toward an "AI-first" content-production model, replacing contractor labor with AI generation. The CEO claimed 4–5x content production with the same headcount (September 2025). User backlash followed, and by Q2 2025 earnings the company described DAU growth as landing at the "lower end of projections."

## Why it matters for the CMM
Canonical Level 2 failure arc alongside Klarna and Shopify. Duolingo exemplifies the specific L2 pattern where AI replaces an existing human workflow without designing the governing protocol for quality signals, user trust, or brand voice. The reversal surfaced not as an internal quality complaint but as a measurable user-behavior signal (DAU growth shortfall) — the protocol gap became visible only through a trailing metric.
