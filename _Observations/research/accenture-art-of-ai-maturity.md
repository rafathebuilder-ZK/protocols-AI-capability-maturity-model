# Accenture — The Art of AI Maturity (2024)

## Summary
Accenture's industry-facing AI maturity study segments firms along Foundation and Differentiation axes, producing four archetypes. Headline figures: 12% are "AI Achievers," 63% are "AI Experimenters." Achievers are distinguished by CEO and senior leadership sponsorship (83%), industrialized tooling, and cross-cutting talent. BBVA is cited as an exemplar with 4,000+ internal custom GPTs deployed. Accenture projects AI transformation will occur 16 months faster than the prior digital transformation cycle.

## Why it matters for the CMM
Cross-level empirical baseline. The 12% / 63% split is the single most-cited anchor for "most organizations are at Level 1 or early Level 2, a minority have crossed into Level 3/4." Accenture's Foundation/Differentiation axis is also directly usable as diagnostic dimensions in Section 4 of the CMM. The CEO-sponsorship finding is a structural signal for Level 4: infrastructural maturity correlates with top-down organizational investment, not individual or team-level adoption.

## Claims citable from this observation
- 12% of firms are AI Achievers; 63% are AI Experimenters
- AI Achievers "are 3.5 times more likely than Experimenters to see their AI-influenced revenue surpass 30% of their total revenues" (verbatim, Accenture)
- Achievers are 25% more likely to scale AI pilots to production
- 83% of Achievers have CEO and senior leadership sponsorship
- BBVA has deployed 4,000+ internal custom GPTs
- AI transformation is projected to happen 16 months faster than the prior digital transformation cycle
