Menlo Ventures: 2025 State of Generative AI in the Enterprise
Source: https://menlovc.com/perspective/2025-the-state-of-generative-ai-in-the-enterprise/
Published: 2025 (survey conducted November 7-25, 2025)
Methodology: ~500 U.S. enterprise AI decision-makers + bottoms-up ecosystem analysis
Note: Excludes chips, traditional cloud inference, and AI features embedded in legacy software

--- KEY FINDINGS ---

MARKET SIZE & GROWTH
- Enterprise GenAI market: $37B in 2025 (up from $11.5B in 2024) — 3.2x YoY increase
- "Fastest-scaling software category in history"
- 6% of global SaaS market within 3 years of ChatGPT's launch

SPENDING DISTRIBUTION
- Application layer: $19B (51%)
  - Departmental AI: $7.3B (coding dominates at $4B)
  - Vertical AI: $3.5B (healthcare leads at $1.5B)
  - Horizontal AI: $8.4B (copilots at 86% share)
- Infrastructure layer: $18B (49%)
  - Foundation model APIs: $12.5B
  - Model training infrastructure: $4B
  - AI infrastructure (storage/orchestration): $1.5B

BUILD VS. BUY SHIFT
- 2024: 47% build / 53% buy
- 2025: 24% build / 76% buy (dramatic reversal)

CONVERSION RATES
- AI buyers convert at 47% vs. SaaS 25%

PRODUCT-LED GROWTH
- PLG = 27% of AI application spend (~4x traditional software rate of 7%)
- Including shadow adoption: ~40% of total application spending

STARTUP VS. INCUMBENT
- Startups captured 63% of application revenue (up from 36% last year)
- Incumbents hold 56% of infrastructure spending

FOUNDATION MODEL MARKET SHARE (enterprise LLM usage)
- Anthropic: 40% (up from 24%)
- OpenAI: 27% (down from 50% in 2023)
- Google: 21% (up from 7%)
- Open-source: 11% (down from 19%)
- Anthropic: ~54% coding market share, up from 42% six months prior (Claude Code)

DEPARTMENTAL AI ADOPTION
- Coding: $4B (55% of departmental AI) — "first true killer use case"
- IT operations: $700M
- Marketing: $660M
- Customer success: $630M
- Design: $510M
- HR: $370M
- 50% of developers use AI coding tools daily; teams report 15%+ velocity gains

ARCHITECTURAL MATURITY
- 16% of enterprise deployments qualify as true agents (27% of startups)
- Dominant approaches: prompt design, RAG, fixed-sequence/routing workflows
- Fine-tuning, tool calling, RL remain niche

2026 PREDICTIONS
1. AI will exceed human performance in practical programming tasks
2. Jevons's paradox: net AI spending rises despite falling inference costs
3. Explainability and governance move mainstream
4. Models shift to edge (on-device deployment)
